Headings & Paragraph

What You Will Learn

  • How headings organize a webpage
  • How paragraph tag displays content
  • How real websites structure text
  • Basic structure practice

1. HTML Headings (h1 to h6)

Headings are used to define titles and sections of a webpage. They help users understand the structure of content easily.

Think of headings like chapters in a book — the main title is big, and sub-topics are smaller.

<h1>Main Title</h1>
<h2>Sub Heading</h2>
<h3>Section Heading</h3>
<h4>Small Heading</h4>
<h5>Smaller Heading</h5>
<h6>Smallest Heading</h6>

Important: Always use only one h1 for main title of page.

2. Paragraph Tag (<p>)

The paragraph tag is used to write normal text content on a webpage. It helps display readable information for users.

Without paragraphs, your content will look messy and hard to understand.

<p>This is a paragraph in HTML</p>

Paragraphs improve readability and make content clean.

3. How Text Works in a Webpage

A webpage is made of structured text:

  • Headings → for titles
  • Paragraphs → for content

Together, they create a clean and understandable layout.

<h1>My Website</h1> <p>Welcome to my first website</p>

4. Real Life Example

  • Heading = Title of a book 📖
  • Paragraph = Content inside book 📄

Without headings and paragraphs, content becomes confusing.

5. Important Points

  • h1 is the most important heading
  • Use headings in proper order (h1 → h2 → h3)
  • Paragraph is used for normal content
  • Headings help in SEO (Google ranking)
